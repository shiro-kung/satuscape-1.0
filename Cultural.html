Dunlayaram Temple

Location:Moo 2, Ban Chin, Chalung Subdistrict, Mueang Satun District, Satun Province

Details:

  • Dunlayaram Temple is a Buddhist temple in Satun, known for its historical and spiritual significance. The temple features classic Thai temple architecture, with a central prayer hall (viharn), a stupa (chedi), and well-maintained grounds. It is a place for visitors to experience Thai religious culture and practices, often hosting local religious ceremonies.

Satun Santayaram Temple

Location:87 Ruangrit Jaroon Road, Phiman Subdistrict, Mueang District, Satun Province

Details:

  • Satun Santayaram Temple is a well-known Buddhist temple in Satun. The temple is a great example of traditional Thai architecture, with a large viharn, chedi, and beautiful gardens. It serves as a center for Buddhist worship and offers a peaceful setting for visitors to reflect, meditate, or participate in local ceremonies.

Arthorn Rangsarit Temple

Location:Kamphaeng Subdistrict, La-ngu District, Satun Province

Details:

Known for its peaceful atmosphere and cultural significance, it features traditional Thai temple architecture and is often a place for meditation, prayer, and community events. Visitors can admire the intricate design of the temple while experiencing the local culture.

Mambang Mosque

Location:Phiman Subdistrict, Mueang District, Satun Province

Details:

  • Mambang Mosque is a mosque located in Satun, serving as a place of worship for the Muslim community. The mosque is an important center for religious activities, including daily prayers, Friday prayers, and special events during Ramadan. Its architecture features traditional Islamic elements, such as a prayer hall, a minaret, and a dome. Visitors can learn about Islamic traditions and engage with the local Muslim community.
